Exercise 2

Use the Deck class from last lab and your modified Card class to complete this exercise.

Create a program that determines the type of poker hand of 5 cards specified on the command-line. The hand will be specified by whitespace-delimited list of card abbreviations in argv[1]. Each card will be represented by up to three characters: the rank and the suit. Name the source ph.cpp and the program ph. For example, the following command would specify Queen of Hearts, 10 of Hearts, 7 of Clubs, 6 of Hearts, and 4 of Spades:

./ph "QH 10H 7C 6H 4S"

The result of your command for the above example would display:

Nothing

Here are the types of poker hands the program should determine (from best hand to worst):

  • Straight flush: Five cards of sequential rank, all of the same suit
  • Four of a kind: Four cards of the same rank and one card of a different rank
  • Full house: Three cards of one rank and two cards of another rank
  • Flush: Five cards of the same suit, not of sequential rank
  • Straight: Five cards of sequential rank, not all of the same suit
  • Three of a kind: Three cards of one rank and two cards of two other ranks
  • Two pair: Two cards of one rank, two cards of a second rank, and 1 card of a third rank
  • One pair: Two cards of one rank and 3 cards of three other ranks
  • Nothing: Five cards not all of sequential rank, not all of the same suit, and no repeated ranks. (A hand that doesn't fall into any of the above categories)
